Denoising Diffusion Models with Multi-Style Decoupling Attention for Controllable Voice Style Transfer

Anonymous

Abstract

Voice style transfer aims to preserve the content of speech while altering attributes such as timbre or emotion. The simultaneous transfer of these attributes has become increasingly important in artificial intelligence development. Despite the notable progress achieved by diffusion-based generative models in voice style transfer, the inherent limitations associated with parameter sharing across different stages of generation pose significant challenges in effectively controlling both timbre and emotion concurrently. To tackle this challenge, we propose a Diffusion model with multi-style decoupling attention for Voice Style Transfer. First, we leverage self-supervised representation to disentangle speech representations. Subsequently, we introduce the Multi-Style Decoupling Attention to facilitate disentanglement guidance, enabling precise and independent control over noise denoising associated with each attribute and yielding fusion representation. Then, the diffusion model uses the fusion representation for style transfer with timbre and emotion attributes. Moreover, we also propose multi-style classifier-free guidance in the sample stage for attribute control, which can realize fine-grained control for emotion attributes and timbre expression. Finally, we demonstrate the superiority of our approach over existing style transfer methods in terms of both timbre and emotion transfer.
